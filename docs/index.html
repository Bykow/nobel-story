Nobel Prizes across countries and ages

The following map shows which country won Nobel Prices over the given range of years. You can play with the slider to adjust the period of time you want to see. The more red the country is, the more Prizes it won.
A laureate usually has an affiliation, mostly universities or research enterprises. I used the country in which this affiliation is. For certain prizes, the laureate has no affiliation, in that case I chose to take in account the country they were born in.
You can also clic on the left and right arrows to see a brief description of a given era. Keep in mind that I chose those years and wrote the text, I’m no genius :)
1901-1938 The beginning

The first years of Nobel prices were almost exclusively distributed between occidental countries. Germany, USA, France and other European countries won those prices mainly because the scientific community was based in Europe at that time.

1938-1945 World War II

During WWII, Europe and thus the Nobel foundation, had less time to distribute the prices for obvious reasons.

1945-1970 After War

After the World War II and the victory of the Allied forces, began an era of prosperity for the world, except for the country part of the Axis (Germany, Italy, Japan,..).

We can see that during those years, the United States of America won most of the Nobel Prices while Germany barely won any.

1970-2000

The baby boom created another wave of American Nobel Prizes, over this 30 years span, most of the laureate were affiliate to the United States of America.

2000-2017 Third Millennial

During the last 17 years, several personalities from a lot of different country have received Nobel Prizes, that can be explained with the expansion of reason that get someone a prize.

1901-2017 The big picture

And that’s is the end of this story, you can see the complete distribution on the map. Thanks for reading !
